The Fragmentation Crisis in Enterprise AI

Every department has built its own model. Every model only does one thing. The result: hundreds of brittle, expensive, siloed systems that fail when conditions change — and nobody can see the full picture.

↓ Click any silo to expose its failure modes
01
Zero Generalization
A model trained on factory sensor A cannot predict anything about sensor B — even if the physics are identical. Every new sensor = a new project from scratch.
02
Catastrophic Forgetting
Retrain a model on new data and it forgets everything it learned before. Organizations must keep old models frozen while new ones are built alongside — doubling cost.
03
Cold Start Failure
Traditional models need months of historical data before making a single useful prediction. New assets, new markets, new facilities? You're flying blind for 6–18 months.
04
Distribution Drift
When operating conditions change — new season, new supplier, new product — model accuracy collapses silently. Teams discover the problem only after costly decisions are made.
05
No Cross-Domain Insight
Your energy model cannot inform your supply chain model. Your maintenance model doesn't know what finance knows. Siloed AI means siloed intelligence — the right hand never knows what the left is doing.
06
Maintenance Explosion
Each model needs its own pipeline, monitoring, retraining schedule, and team. 50 models = 50 pipelines. Organizations report spending 70%+ of ML budgets on maintenance, not innovation.
